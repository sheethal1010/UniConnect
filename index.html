Platform Overview

🎓
Product Purpose
UniConnect unifies five student-centric workflows — study partnerships, project teams, micro-freelance, resource sharing, and space booking — into a single authenticated campus platform.
👥
Target Users
Undergraduate and graduate students, campus administrators, and faculty. All users authenticate via university SSO to ensure campus-only access and academic integrity.
📐
Design Approach
Low-fidelity wireframes using ASCII diagrams. Each screen is documented with components, interactions, navigation flow, and edge cases. Figma-recreatable by design.
🗺️
Architecture
Hub-and-spoke model. Dashboard is the central hub. Five feature modules (Study, Projects, Freelance, Resources, Spaces) radiate outward. Notifications and messaging are global.
📱
Platform Scope
Desktop-first responsive web application. All 14 wireframes represent desktop (1280px+) viewport. Mobile variants share the same component library with collapsed navigation.
⚙️
Admin Scope
Dedicated admin panel for space management, user moderation, flagged content review, and platform analytics. Role-based access separates admin from student views.

05

End-to-End User Journey

PERSONA
Maya — 2nd Year Computer Science Student
Maya has just started a new semester. She needs a study partner for Algorithms, wants to form a team for her capstone, and is looking to earn extra money by offering web design help to other students.
STEP 01 — DISCOVERY
Maya discovers UniConnect via campus email
She clicks the link in the university newsletter, arrives at the Landing Page. She reads the feature overview and social proof (1,200+ students matched). She clicks "Get Started Free."
S01 Landing Page
STEP 02 — AUTHENTICATION
Maya creates her account
She's redirected to Sign Up. She enters her university email ([email protected]). The system validates the .edu domain. She sets a password and verifies via email. She's redirected back to a welcome screen.
S02a LoginS02b Sign Up
STEP 03 — ONBOARDING
Maya builds her profile (3-step flow)
Step 1: Major (CS), year (2nd), skills (React, Python, Figma). Step 2: Availability calendar (Mon–Fri 2pm–6pm, weekend mornings). Step 3: Interests (algorithms, startups, design). She sees a completion progress bar and finishes in 4 minutes.
S03 Profile Creation
STEP 04 — ORIENTATION
Maya lands on the Dashboard
She sees her personalized dashboard: "3 study partner matches ready," "2 open project teams need your skills," and "The library Room B is available now." She explores each module via the hub cards.
S04 Dashboard
STEP 05 — STUDY MATCHING
Maya finds a study partner for Algorithms
She opens Study Partner Matching, filters by "CS 301 - Algorithms" and "Available Tuesday 3pm." She sees 5 compatible students. She reviews Alex's profile (same course, same study style: collaborative). She sends a "Study Request" — Alex gets a notification.
S05 Study MatchingS10 NotificationsS11 Profile
STEP 06 — PROJECT FORMATION
Maya posts and joins a capstone team
She navigates to Projects, clicks "Post a Project." She describes her capstone idea (an AI campus app), specifies she needs 1 backend dev and 1 UI designer. Within 2 hours she has 3 applicants. She reviews profiles and forms her team of 3.
S06 Project FormationS11 Profile
STEP 07 — FREELANCE LISTING
Maya offers web design services
She goes to Freelance Marketplace and posts "Logo + landing page design – $40 flat." She sets delivery time (3 days), adds portfolio screenshots. A student from the Business School finds her listing and sends a request. Maya accepts, they agree on scope, funds go into escrow.
S07 Freelance MarketplaceS10 Messages
STEP 08 — RESOURCE SHARING
Maya finds and borrows class notes
Before her Algorithms midterm, she searches Resources for "CS 301 notes." She finds handwritten notes from a 3rd-year student uploaded as PDF. She downloads them for free. She also uploads her own Linear Algebra notes to pay it forward.
S08 Resource Sharing
STEP 09 — SPACE BOOKING
Maya books a study room for her team
She opens Spaces, views the live campus map. Room 3B in the Engineering Library is available Thursday 2–4pm. She selects the time slot, confirms the booking, and sends the booking confirmation to her project team via Messages.
S09 Space BookingS10 Messages
STEP 10 — ONGOING USE
Maya manages notifications and profile
Over the week she receives: study request reply from Alex, team project update, freelance completion with 5-star review. Her profile now shows completed tasks, ratings, and an active study partnership. The dashboard updates to reflect her activity and recommends new spaces.
S10 NotificationsS11 ProfileS04 Dashboard
